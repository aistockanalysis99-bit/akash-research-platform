# Akash Research Platform — Your Guide

*A simple walkthrough of what this system does, how it works, and how it helps you make better investment decisions.*

---

## 1. What this is, in one minute

Think of this platform as a **full-time research team that never sleeps**, working only for your portfolio.

Every trading day it:

1. **Scans the whole US market** to find stocks with real momentum behind them.
2. **Deep-researches** each promising stock the way a professional investment firm would — looking at the business, the news, the charts, and what big institutions are doing.
3. **Argues both sides** (why to buy, why to avoid) and reaches a clear verdict.
4. **Sends you the verdict** in plain language on Telegram.
5. **You decide** what to actually buy. Nothing happens to your money without you.

It does not gamble. It does not trade on its own. It is a **decision-support system** — it does the heavy research, and hands you a clear, well-reasoned recommendation so *you* can make the final call with confidence.

---

## 2. The three parts of the platform

The website has three sections at the top: **Portfolio**, **AI Analysis**, and **Backtesting**. Here is what each one is for.

### 🗂️ Portfolio — "What I own"

This is your real account, mirrored on screen.

- It shows every stock you hold, what you paid, what it's worth now, and your profit or loss.
- It tracks your **cash** and your **total account value** (cash + the value of your holdings).
- **You add positions manually.** When you buy a stock in your real brokerage account, you enter it here (ticker, number of shares, price, date) so the system knows what you own.
- There is **no spending limit** — it simply mirrors your real account, whatever size it is.

> **Why this matters:** The AI uses your real holdings to give smarter advice. If you already own a lot of tech stocks, it will warn you before you buy *more* tech and become dangerously concentrated.

### 🤖 AI Analysis — "Should I buy this?"

This is the brain of the system. For any stock, it runs a team of **11 specialist AI analysts**, then reaches a verdict.

The team works in three stages:

**Stage 1 — Six analysts study the stock at the same time:**

| Analyst | What it looks at | In plain words |
|---|---|---|
| **Fundamental** | Revenue, profit, debt, cash | "Is this a healthy, growing business?" |
| **News** | Recent news, analyst ratings, insider activity | "What's happening lately? Are insiders buying or selling?" |
| **Technical** | Price charts and trends | "Is the stock price in an uptrend or falling?" |
| **Institutional Flow** | What big funds are doing | "Is smart money buying or quietly getting out?" |
| **Options Structure** | Activity in the options market | "Where do the big traders expect the price to go?" |
| **Macro Regime** | The overall market mood | "Is the whole market calm and rising, or nervous and falling?" |

**Stage 2 — A structured debate:**

- A **Bull** analyst builds the strongest possible case to *buy*.
- A **Bear** analyst builds the strongest possible case to *avoid*.
- A **Judge** weighs the two arguments.
- A **Risk Manager** applies hard safety rules (e.g. "never buy right before an earnings report").

**Stage 3 — The final decision:**

- A **Portfolio Manager** (the most capable AI) takes everything above, compares it against *what you already own*, and issues one clear verdict:

| Verdict | What it means for you |
|---|---|
| 🟢 **BUY** | Strong opportunity. Worth adding. |
| ⚠️ **WATCH** | Good company, but not the right moment or price. Wait. |
| 🔴 **AVOID** | The risks outweigh the rewards right now. |

It also gives a **confidence score from 1 to 10**, a **price target**, and a **suggested "cut your losses" price** so you always know your downside.

### 📊 Backtesting — "Does the strategy actually work?"

Before trusting any stock-picking method with real money, you want proof it has worked in the past. That's what Backtesting does.

- It takes the system's stock-picking rules and **replays them across years of real market history**.
- It shows you: *"If we had followed these exact rules over the last X years, here's how the money would have grown, how often it won, and the worst drop it would have suffered."*
- This is the **engine room** of the system. The same rules it proves here are the rules that scan the market for you every evening.

> **Why this matters:** You're not trusting a hunch. You're trusting a method that has been tested against history before it ever touches your portfolio.

## 3. How it all fits together — the journey of a stock

Here is the complete path, from "thousands of stocks" down to "a decision on your phone." Think of it as a **funnel** that filters out noise at every step:

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  THE WHOLE MARKET  (100 large US companies)   │
   └───────────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                           │
              STEP 1: The Strategy scans them all
              (momentum + trend + breakout math)
                           │
                           ▼
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  TOP SIGNALS  (~10 strongest stocks today)    │   ← only these pass
   └───────────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                           │
              STEP 2: The 11-analyst AI team
              deeply researches each one
                           │
                           ▼
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  CLEAR VERDICTS  (BUY / WATCH / AVOID)         │
   │  with confidence, price target, and stop      │
   └───────────────────────┬───────────────────────┘
                           │
              STEP 3: Sent to your phone (Telegram)
                           │
                           ▼
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │  YOU DECIDE  → add to Portfolio if you agree   │   ← you are always in control
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**The key idea:** the strategy decides *what is worth looking at*, the AI decides *whether it's actually a good idea right now*, and **you decide what to do**. Each layer removes weak ideas so that only the best, most-researched opportunities reach you.

> **What could go wrong:** The stock is expensive, and growth is slowing. If next quarter slows further, it could drop 25–30%.

> **The trade:**

> • 6-month target: $370 (about +20%)
> • Cut losses at: $283 (about −8%)
>
> **Exit early if:** sales growth drops below 20% on the next earnings report.

You don't need to understand the math behind it. You get: **what it is, why it's interesting, what could go wrong, and exactly what to do.**

## 7. How this makes you a better investor

Beyond the daily convenience, here is the deeper value:

- **Discipline replaces emotion.** The same rigorous process runs on every stock, every day — whether the market is euphoric or panicking. That consistency is what separates professional investors from amateurs.

- **You always see the bear case.** Most people only hear why a stock is great (usually from someone trying to sell it). Here, a dedicated Bear analyst hunts for every reason *not* to buy — so you go in with eyes open.

- **Risk is built in, not bolted on.** Every recommendation comes with a price target *and* a "cut your losses" level. You always know your downside before you commit a dollar.

- **It considers your whole portfolio, not just one stock.** A great stock can still be a bad buy if you already own five just like it. The system catches that.

- **It learns.** After a position is closed, the system writes down the lesson and applies it to future decisions — so it gets sharper over time.

- **It respects your time.** A professional analyst night of work — reading filings, news, and institutional data on a dozen companies — is delivered to your phone as a few clear messages.

## 8. Simple glossary

A few words you'll see, in plain English:

| Term | What it really means |
|---|---|
| **Momentum** | A stock whose price has been steadily climbing. The strategy favors winners that keep winning. |
| **Breakout** | The price just pushed above its recent ceiling — often the start of a bigger move. |
| **Conviction (1–10)** | How sure the AI is. Higher = stronger opinion. It will not recommend a full buy below 5. |
| **Watchlist** | The list of stocks the system keeps researching for you, even if you don't own them yet. |
| **Position** | A stock you own. |
| **Stop / "cut losses at"** | The price at which you should sell to limit a loss if the trade goes wrong. |
| **Backtest** | Testing the strategy against past market history to prove it works before using it live. |
| **Regime** | The overall mood of the market — calm and rising ("risk-on") or nervous and falling ("risk-off"). |
